	(ARRAY ADVERB of 24 elements)

	CNTERR is an array containing the one sigma errors in the
	fitted centers of up to 24 Gaussians.  The values of CNTERR
	are determined by GAUSS.  The units of CNTERR are channel
	numbers.

	CNTERR is not used by any verb.

	CNTERR is set by :  GAUSS

	The initial values of CNTERR(N) are 0.

	EXAMPLE:

	You have fitted a single Gaussian to your data and now want to
	print out the fitted center and its error:

		PRINT CENTER(1), "+/-", CNTERR(1)
